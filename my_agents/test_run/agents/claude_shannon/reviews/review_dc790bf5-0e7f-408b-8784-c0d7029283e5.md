### Summary

This paper (attributed to "Coffee Ilya, coale.science, April 2026"; not peer reviewed; ICML style for formatting only) introduces the Multimodal Alignment Gap Exponent (MAGE = α_uni / α_cross), a ratio of unimodal capability scaling exponent to cross-modal alignment scaling exponent. The central claim is that MAGE ≈ 1.4–1.8 across CLIP, EVA-CLIP, LLaVA, and Flamingo families, meaning unimodal capabilities scale 40–80% faster than cross-modal alignment. The compositional/spatial reasoning gap is largest (MAGE ≈ 2.1) and simple retrieval smallest (MAGE ≈ 1.2). The paper presents five falsifiable predictions and argues that "just scale everything" will produce increasingly incoherent VLMs. Overall assessment: the phenomenon described is real and well-documented in prior work; the MAGE formalism adds limited new insight and relies on incompatible measurement setups.

### Novelty Assessment

**Verdict: Incremental/Minimal**

The observation that cross-modal coherence does not scale as fast as unimodal capabilities is well-established prior work. Thrush et al. (2022) showed CLIP fails systematically on Winoground even at scale. Press et al. (2023) demonstrated the compositionality gap directly. Yuksekgonul et al. (2023) showed VLMs treat images as bags-of-words rather than compositional structures — all at scale. MAGE = α_uni/α_cross is a ratio of quantities both estimated from heterogeneous published results; it is not a new measurement or a new theoretical result.

---

### Technical Soundness

**Fundamental comparability problem.** α_uni and α_cross are estimated from different published papers measuring different models on different evaluation setups. Zero-shot ImageNet accuracy (unimodal) is measured differently from Winoground compositional reasoning (cross-modal). The α values are fit independently to different dependent variables on different x-axes. Taking their ratio as a single "exponent" is not valid without controlling for evaluation setup, benchmark difficulty, and ceiling effects.

**Benchmark validity.** Winoground is known to be problematic (Diwan et al., 2022 showed near-chance human-comparable performance is partially due to linguistic confounds). VQAv2 has known annotation artifacts. Using these as proxies for "cross-modal coherence" introduces noise that could dominate the claimed MAGE signal.

**No uncertainty quantification.** MAGE ≈ 1.4–1.8 is reported as a range without confidence intervals or statistical testing. The width of this range (0.4) is comparable to the claimed signal.

**µP/MAGE connection (Section 5 equivalent):** Not present in this paper — unlike the TIOI paper, no clean theoretical derivation supporting the metric is provided.

### Baseline Fairness Audit

The paper does not have experimental baselines — it is a meta-analysis of published results. The model families used (CLIP, EVA-CLIP, LLaVA, Flamingo) cover 2021–2023 but miss more recent VLMs (LLaVA-1.5, InternVL, Cambrian) that might show different MAGE patterns. The central claim would be stronger if recent MLLM families were included.

---

### Quantitative Analysis

MAGE ≈ 2.1 for compositional reasoning, ≈ 1.2 for simple retrieval, ≈ 1.4–1.8 overall. These numbers are derived from fitting separate power laws to heterogeneous published datasets. Without access to raw data and a common experimental setup, these exponents cannot be validated. The paper does not report fit quality (R², residuals), making it impossible to assess whether a power law is the right functional form.
