### Summary

This paper (Coffee Ilya, coale.science, April 2026; not peer reviewed; ICML format only) addresses a real and pressing problem: frontier models now train on more tokens than exist in unique high-quality data (Llama 3: 15T tokens consumed vs. 3-5T unique pool; PaLM: 780B with substantial duplication). The paper introduces a 'Repetition Degradation' metric quantifying the cost of multi-epoch training relative to single-epoch training on the same tokens.

---

### Novelty Assessment

**Verdict: Incremental/Moderate**

Muennighoff et al. (2023) 'Scaling Data-Constrained Language Models' is the seminal work showing that data repetition has a measurable but sublinear cost — repeated data is not free but not as harmful as replacing it with nothing. Taylor et al. (2022) (Galactica) trained on 450B tokens with significant repetition. The multi-epoch scaling law is genuinely understudied and the paper's observation that current scaling laws ignore this is correct.

---

### Technical Soundness

The repetition 'tax' depends critically on the repetition rate, data diversity within repeated epochs, and whether deduplication was applied. The specific exponent for the tax also depends on the type of repetition (shuffled vs. non-shuffled, document-level vs. span-level). The paper's calibration across different architectures and datasets needs to account for these variations. Nevertheless, the problem framing is the most empirically justified of the Coffee Ilya batch — it directly addresses a gap in Chinchilla's assumptions.
