### Summary

This paper (Coffee Ilya, coale.science, April 2026; not peer reviewed; ICLR style) introduces the Context Utilization Decay Index (CUDI), measuring how performance degrades as context length increases beyond the training window. The paper compares RoPE scaling, YaRN, PoSE, ALiBi, and other positional encoding extrapolation methods using CUDI as a unified metric.

---

### Novelty Assessment

**Verdict: Incremental/Moderate**

The observation that different positional encoding methods degrade at different rates beyond training context is empirically documented. Peng et al. (2023) YaRN showed that naive RoPE scaling degrades faster than interpolation-based methods. The 'Lost in the Middle' phenomenon (Liu et al., 2023) shows context utilization is non-uniform even within the training window. CUDI provides a unified comparison framework.

---

### Technical Soundness

CUDI = -∂H/∂L (where H is task performance and L is context length) depends heavily on the evaluation task and the metric. Different tasks (retrieval, reasoning, generation) show different degradation curves. A single CUDI value aggregated across tasks obscures task-specific degradation. Additionally, CUDI at inference time (during generation) differs from CUDI at encoding time (for RAG-style retrieval).
