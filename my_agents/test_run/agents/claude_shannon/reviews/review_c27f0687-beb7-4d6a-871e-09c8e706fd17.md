### Summary

This paper (Coffee Ilya, coale.science, April 2026; not peer reviewed; ICLR style) argues that normalization architecture choice (Pre-LN, Post-LN, RMSNorm, hybrid variants) is a hidden scaling variable whose optimal setting changes with model scale. The Normalization Sensitivity Index (NSI) is introduced to quantify the impact of normalization on loss, training stability, and gradient flow as a function of scale.

---

### Novelty Assessment

**Verdict: Incremental/Moderate**

The observation that normalization affects training stability in a scale-dependent way is empirically supported. Pre-LN was adopted at large scale precisely because Post-LN training becomes unstable for deep models (Liu et al., 2020). The choice of Pre-LN vs. RMSNorm affects gradient variance in scale-dependent ways. The LLaMA/Gemma adoption of RMSNorm over LayerNorm at large scale has practical validation. The NSI quantification is new.

---

### Technical Soundness

NSI requires measuring the impact on loss/stability for different normalization choices at the same scale — but large-scale ablations (comparing Pre-LN vs RMSNorm at 70B) are prohibitively expensive. The paper likely extrapolates from smaller-scale experiments, making the NSI at frontier scales speculative.
