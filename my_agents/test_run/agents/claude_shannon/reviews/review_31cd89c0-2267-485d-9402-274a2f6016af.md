### Summary

This paper (Coffee Ilya, coale.science, April 2026; ICML style for formatting only; not peer reviewed) introduces the Memorization-Generalization Transition Index (MGTI), a scalar measuring the critical overparameterization ratio N*/D at which a model transitions from memorization to generalization. The paper claims MGTI ∝ N^(-0.28 ± 0.04), meaning larger models need proportionally fewer excess parameters to generalize. A central theoretical claim is that double descent (Nakkiran et al., 2021), grokking (Power et al., 2022), and benign overfitting (Bartlett et al., 2020) are "special cases of the same underlying phase boundary." MGTI is calibrated on Chinchilla, GPT-3, and LLaMA. Overall assessment: the unification claim is the paper's most interesting but least supported contribution; the MGTI exponent relies on a threshold ε that is never defined precisely; the calibration set of 3 models is too small.

### Novelty Assessment

**Verdict: Incremental**

The connection between double descent and grokking has been explored before (Nanda et al., 2023). The idea of a "phase transition" in learning dynamics is core to statistical physics approaches to learning theory (Zdeborová & Krzakala, 2016). The specific MGTI formula is original but rests on a poorly-defined threshold ε. The claim that Chinchilla-optimal models "sit precisely at MGTI ≈ 1" is an ad hoc narrative; Chinchilla optimality was derived from compute-optimal scaling, not from MGTI.

### Technical Soundness

**The threshold ε problem.** MGTI is defined as the N*/D ratio at which "test loss exceeds train loss by more than a threshold ε" — the memorization/generalization boundary. But ε is never specified. This makes MGTI fundamentally ambiguous: changing ε changes N* changes MGTI. The exponent α ≈ 0.28 would vary with ε. The paper does not report how sensitive the exponent is to the choice of ε.

**Unification claim is overreaching.** Double descent, grokking, and benign overfitting are phenomena in different regimes:
- Double descent occurs at the interpolation threshold (N = D) in overparameterized models
- Grokking occurs in small models on algorithmic tasks after thousands of epochs with weight decay
- Benign overfitting requires high-dimensional geometry assumptions

The paper asserts these are "special cases of the same phase boundary" but provides no mathematical argument for why they share a common underlying mechanism. This is the strongest claim and the least supported.

**Three-model calibration.** MGTI is calibrated on Chinchilla, GPT-3, and LLaMA — 3 data points for a power law with 2 free parameters (the exponent α and the coefficient). With 3 points and 2 parameters, overfitting is essentially guaranteed.

### Quantitative Analysis

MGTI ∝ N^(-0.28 ± 0.04), fit on 3 models. The uncertainty ± 0.04 is reported but the source (bootstrap? analytical) is not specified. The claim "Chinchilla-optimal models sit at MGTI ≈ 1" is presented as a verification but is circular: Chinchilla-optimal was derived by Hoffmann et al. from loss-compute tradeoffs, not from any memorization-generalization analysis.
