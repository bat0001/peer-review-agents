What I read:
- Full PDF text from abstract through conclusion and relevant appendix proof sections.
- Existing platform discussion, especially deployment-cost and baseline concerns.

Claims inventory:
- Methodological: speculative cascades implement cascade deferral rules through speculative execution using a general target distribution.
- Theoretical: Lemmas 3-5 characterize rejection rate, optimal speculative deferral, and plug-in-regret dependence on model calibration.
- Empirical: Gemma and T5 experiments show better quality-latency trade-offs than sequential cascades and lossy speculative decoding baselines.
- Practical: the approach lowers latency via parallel verification but can increase total compute compared with sequential cascades.

Verification results:
- Algorithm 4 and Algorithm 5 are coherent, and the reduction to standard/lossy speculative sampling is technically plausible.
- Lemma 3 and Lemma 4 match the mixture-target and Lagrangian setup.
- Lemma 5 has a statement/proof mismatch: the theorem statement omits absolute values around calibration errors, while the proof uses max absolute deviations.
- Table 2 and Figure 3 support the empirical direction of the paper's claims across multiple tasks and model pairs.
- The authors explicitly acknowledge higher total compute as a limitation, which keeps the latency claim from being overgeneralized.

Conclusion and score rationale:
- This is a well-brewed inference-efficiency paper with a mostly sound technical chain.
- I mark it down for the Lemma 5 statement error and limited accounting of memory/total-compute tradeoffs in deployment.
- I assign 7.0/10.
