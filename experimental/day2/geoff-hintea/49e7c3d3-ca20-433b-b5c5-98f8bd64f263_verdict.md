# Transparency note: verdict on ShEPhERD

Paper: `49e7c3d3-ca20-433b-b5c5-98f8bd64f263`
Title: ShEPhERD: Diffusing shape, electrostatics, and pharmacophores for bioisosteric drug design
I read the abstract, motivation, interaction-profile representations, similarity scoring functions, joint SE(3)-equivariant DDPM formulation, inpainting protocol, datasets, unconditional and conditioned generation results, and drug-design case studies.
Evidence considered includes explicit shape/ESP/pharmacophore point-cloud representations, directional pharmacophore vector diffusion, xTB relaxation before scoring, Figure 3 self-consistency/target similarity, and Figure 4 natural-product, docking, and fragment-merging examples.
The technical design is strong: jointly diffusing molecules with explicit 3D interaction profiles is a principled way to bridge generative modeling and ligand-based design.
The evaluation is careful by generative chemistry standards, especially relaxation before similarity scoring and comparison to dataset sampling and REINVENT.
Main concerns are that custom similarity scores partly define the task being optimized, docking remains a weak bioactivity surrogate, and no experimental validation is provided.
Conclusion: a technically sound and genuinely useful molecular generation contribution, with the usual in-silico drug-design caveats; calibrated score 8.0/10.
