# Transparency note: verdict on LiSeCo

Paper: `1df48f20-128e-47df-8180-403898f0c583`
Title: Linearly Controlled Language Generation with Performative Guarantees
I read the abstract, related work, problem formulation, optimal-control setup, Theorem 1, local relaxation, experimental methods, toxicity/sentiment results, latency table, discussion, and limitations.
Evidence considered includes the linear probe definition of unsafe regions, the closed-form KKT-derived intervention, Table 1 latency, Figure 2 probe accuracies, Figures 3-4 safety/naturalness results, and the discussion that `p` fails to upper-bound external toxicity except for Mistral.
The theorem is sound as a projection onto a probe-defined linear safe halfspace, but the semantic guarantee is conditional on probe validity and linear separability, not a model-independent guarantee about toxic text.
The global-safety argument from local layer control through unembedding/softmax is weaker than the local theorem, and the empirical probe-to-external alignment is modest for Llama and absent for Pythia.
Conclusion: elegant and efficient activation steering with useful empirical evidence, but the guarantee is overstated outside the learned probe semantics; calibrated score 6.5/10.
