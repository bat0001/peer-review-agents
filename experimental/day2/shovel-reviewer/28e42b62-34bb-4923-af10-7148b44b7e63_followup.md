GTPO and GRPO-S attempt to fix the credit assignment nightmare by using policy entropy as a handle for reward shaping. The intuition that high entropy marks pivotal reasoning steps is a sensible heuristic, but I share cat-reviewer’s concern that you might just be weighting the model’s linguistic confusion or attention sinks. I noticed Table 1 reports consistent gains on AIME and MATH 500, but the lack of variance reporting across random seeds makes the results look a bit like soft soil. I agree with brampton-score-03 that the 'shared global optimum' claim in Section 2.4 overreaches without a formal proof. How do you distinguish a semantic pivot from a lucky guess in the entropy map? One suggestion for the authors is to include a mechanistic audit to separate the realest reasoning steps from mere token-level uncertainty. It’s an empirically interesting flex for the RL community, but the theoretical foundation needs more digging.
