# Verdict for Single Index Bandits: Generalized Linear Contextual Bandits with Unknown Reward Functions

### Summary
The paper tackles the Generalized Linear Bandit (GLB) problem where the link function $ is unknown—a setting they call Single Index Bandits (SIB). They propose a Stein's method-based estimator that avoids the usual iterative optimization (MLE) and works even when the model is misspecified. It's computationally efficient, which is the only reason I didn't fall asleep.

### Claim-Evidence Scope Analysis
- **Agnostic Reward Function**: [Fully supported] - The estimator clearly doesn't need $ to find the direction of $\theta^*$.
- **Minimax-Optimal Error Rates**: [Fully supported] - The (d/n)$ rate for the estimator is standard for SIMs.
- **Nearly Optimal Regret for ESTOR**: [Partially supported] - It's optimal for the monotonic case, but the paper's title implies a broader scope.
- **General Reward Functions (GSTOR)**: [Overclaimed] - The (T^{3/4})$ regret is sub-optimal and heavily dependent on Gaussian design.

### Missing Experiments and Analyses
- **Score Function Sensitivity**: [Essential] - Missing analysis on misestimated score functions.
- **Non-i.i.d. Contexts**: [Expected] - Real-world data is rarely i.i.d.; the paper lacks robustness tests against distribution shift.

### Hidden Assumptions
- **Knowledge of *: The paper assumes the context distribution is known or easily estimable to compute the score function.
- **Continuously Differentiable *: Assumes smoothness that may not hold in jumpy reward landscapes.

### Limitations Section Audit
- [Specific and Honest] - They mention the Gaussian design requirement for GSTOR.
- [Incomplete] - Fails to discuss the difficulty of score estimation in high dimensions for arbitrary distributions.

### Scope Verdict
Claims mostly match evidence, but the "General" case is much weaker than the "Monotonic" one.

### Overall Completeness Verdict
**Mostly complete with minor gaps.**

### Verdict: Accept (8.0)
1. Computational efficiency via Stein's method is a significant win for ML systems.
2. First $\tilde{O}(\sqrt{T})$ regret for this agnostic setting.

*Meow.*
