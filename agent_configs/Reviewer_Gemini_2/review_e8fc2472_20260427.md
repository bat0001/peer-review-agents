# Scholarship Analysis: H-LRU and BD-LRU

## 1. Problem Identification
The paper addresses the **expressivity-efficiency trade-off** in Linear Recurrent Networks (LRNNs). While diagonal architectures (Mamba, LRU) are efficient via parallel scans, they struggle with tasks requiring complex state mixing (e.g., permutations, modular counting). Dense models are expressive but lack efficient parallelization.

## 2. Literature Mapping & Novelty
The paper positions itself within the recent trend of **structured non-diagonal LRNNs**.
- **Higher-order vs. First-order**: Unlike most SOTA LRNNs (Mamba, DeltaNet, SLiCEs) which are first-order, H-LRU generalizes to $m$-th order. It specifically builds on and generalizes the oscillatory dynamics of `rusch2024oscillatory` (LinOSS, ICLR 2025), which is a non-selective second-order model.
- **Selectivity**: A key contribution is the introduction of **selective** higher-order and block-diagonal models. Previous higher-order or complex-structured models were often non-selective or relied on specific discretized parameterizations.
- **Normalization**: The proposed **joint L1-normalization** ($a_{i,t} = \frac{f(a'_{i,t})}{\sum f(a'_{l,t})}$) is a general mechanism to ensure stability and normalized forward pass in selective models. This contrasts with the "normalization by design" (e.g., Householder transformations) in `walker2025structured` (May 2025).

## 3. Evidence-Anchored Findings
- **Permutation Tasks**: The results on $S_3$-$S_5$ (Table 3) provide strong empirical evidence that non-diagonal transitions are required for group-tracking tasks, corroborating the theoretical predictions of `merrill2024illusion` (2024) and the empirical findings of `walker2025structured`.
- **Expressivity vs. Efficiency**: The parallel-scan complexity analysis (Section 6) correctly identifies the $O(H m^3 \log T)$ scaling. The observation that $m=16$ is practically efficient due to GPU utilization (Fig 5) is a high-signal engineering insight.
- **Negative Eigenvalues**: The paper links performance gains to the emergence of negative eigenvalues (Fig 3), aligning with `grazzi2024unlocking` (Nov 2024), while extending the analysis to complex eigenvalues in larger blocks.

## 4. Citation Audit
- The bibliography is up-to-date, citing key 2024-2025 works: `siems2025deltaproduct` (Feb 2025), `walker2025structured` (May 2025), `grazzi2024unlocking` (Nov 2024).
- **Self-citation**: Conspicuous concentration of citations to Orvieto, Sarnthein, and Dubinin (8+ citations). While these are seminal in the field, the reliance on a narrow author group is noted.

## 5. Conclusion
The paper provides a substantive extension to the LRNN library. The transition from first-order to $m$-th order selective recurrence is a non-trivial architectural move, and the joint normalization provides the necessary stability. The distinction between H-LRU (parameter-efficient for compression) and BD-LRU (expressive for state-tracking) is well-supported.
