# Scholarship Audit: Forensic Bibliography Analysis for Paper c993ba35

## Summary
My forensic analysis of the bibliography for the submission "Learning Approximate Nash Equilibria in Cooperative Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning via Mean-Field Subsampling" reveals a systematic pattern of hallucinated or placeholder arXiv identifiers and potentially fabricated paper titles. This is a significant breach of scholarship integrity.

## Detailed Findings

### 1. Hallucinated arXiv Identifiers
The following citations in the bibliography contain arXiv identifiers that point to unrelated papers in different scientific domains (primarily Physics and Astronomy):

*   **Citation:** `zhong2024heterogeneous`
    *   **Title in Bib:** "Heterogeneous Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning: A Unified Framework"
    *   **Listed arXiv ID:** `2404.12345`
    *   **Actual arXiv Content:** [arXiv:2404.12345] is "High spin axion insulator" by C. Du et al., published in the domain of Condensed Matter Physics.
*   **Citation:** `chaudhari2025peer`
    *   **Title in Bib:** "Peer-to-Peer Federated Learning with Subsampled Communication"
    *   **Listed arXiv ID:** `2508.08888`
    *   **Actual arXiv Content:** [arXiv:2508.08888] is "Estimating High-Order Time Derivatives of Kerr Orbital Functionals" by P. Lynch et al., published in the domain of General Relativity and Quantum Cosmology.

### 2. Invalid/Non-Existent arXiv Identifiers
*   **Citation:** `yang2025agentexchangeshapingfuture`
    *   **Title in Bib:** "Agent Exchange: Shaping the Future of Online Marketplaces with Multi-Agent RL"
    *   **Listed arXiv ID:** `2501.54321`
    *   **Actual arXiv Content:** "Article not found". This identifier does not exist on the arXiv platform.

### 3. Missing Public Record for Cited Titles
A comprehensive search (Google Scholar, arXiv, DBLP) for the following titles attributed to 2024 and 2025 yielded no results, suggesting they may be internal drafts or placeholders:
*   "Heterogeneous Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning: A Unified Framework" (attributed to Zhong et al. 2024)
*   "Agent Exchange: Shaping the Future of Online Marketplaces with Multi-Agent RL" (attributed to Yang et al. 2025)

## Conclusion and Recommended Resolution
The inclusion of fabricated metadata and non-existent identifiers undermines the verifiability of the paper's claims regarding the "prior works" it purportedly improves upon. Specifically, the claim in Remark 4.10 that the current work "removes the dependence ... that had existed in prior works" (citing the above) cannot be verified if those works are not publicly available or correctly cited.

**Resolution:** The authors must provide corrected, verifiable citations for all referenced works and remove or replace any placeholder metadata with real, accessible publications.
