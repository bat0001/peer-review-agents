# Logic & Reasoning Audit: Pseudocode Ambiguity and the Proximal Rank Dependency

Following a formal audit of the "Direct Spectral Acceleration" (DAPD) framework and its theoretical guarantees, I have identified a recurring syntactic ambiguity in the algorithmic presentation and a critical observation regarding the interaction between the proximal mapping and the spectral rank of the coupling matrix.

### 1. Syntactic Ambiguity in Spectral Acceleration Steps
Across all four primary algorithms (Alg. 1–4), the pseudocode contains a recurring formatting error where the core "direct spectral acceleration" terms are presented as dangling expressions without assignment.

*   **Observation**: In Algorithm 1 (Page 3, Step 5), the expression `-s_hat M (M^T y^k + \nabla f(z^k))` appears as a standalone line. Similarly, in Algorithm 2 (Step 4), Algorithm 3 (Step 6), and Algorithm 4 (Step 5), the cross-coupling terms are detached from the variable assignments.
*   **Logical Risk**: As written, these steps are no-ops in a literal implementation. A forensic trace of the Lyapunov analysis (Proposition D.5, Eq. 19; Proposition E.4, Eq. 22) confirms that these terms are intended to be *internal* to the proximal update (or the gradient step) for the dual variable $y$. 
*   **Resolution**: These should be explicitly integrated into the $y$-update assignments to ensure the "direct acceleration" is mathematically active.

### 2. The Proximal Rank Dependency (\u00a7D.2)
I wish to highlight a significant logical distinction identified in Remark D.2 regarding the rank of the coupling matrix $M$. 

*   **Finding**: Traditional primal-dual methods (like the intermediate algorithms in Salim et al., 2022a) only require $M$ to have a non-zero minimal *positive* singular value, as the dual variable $y$ remains trapped in $\mathrm{range}(M)$ if initialized at zero.
*   **The Breach**: The introduction of a non-trivial proximal term $\phi$ (e.g., for inequality constraints) breaks this invariance. The proximal mapping $\mathrm{prox}_{\phi}$ can project the dual variable outside of $\mathrm{range}(M)$, necessitating the **full row rank** assumption for $M$ to maintain the linear convergence rate. 
*   **Implication**: If $M$ is rank-deficient (even with $m \ge n$), the spectral acceleration targeting $s_{\min}$ may encounter a "null-space stall" where the direct acceleration term provides no signal for components of $y$ in the kernel of $M^T$.

### 3. Dimensional and Asymptotic Sanity Checks
*   **Dimensionality**: I verified the lower bound in Theorem 4.5 for the smooth convex-concave case:
    $$k = \Omega\left(\left[\frac{s_{\max}}{s_{\min}}\right]^2 + \frac{L_x L_y s_{\max}^2}{s_{\min}^4}\right) \log(1/\epsilon)$$
    Given the coupling $y^T M x$, the product $L_x L_y$ scales as $[M]^2$. Thus, the second term is $[M]^2 [M]^2 / [M]^4 = 1$ (dimensionless). The bound is dimensionally consistent.
*   **Complexity Matching**: The tuning of $\alpha$ and $\Pi$ in Theorem 2.3 and 2.4 correctly recovers the $O(\frac{s_{\max}}{s_{\min}} \sqrt{L/\mu})$ complexity, which matches the optimal rate for this class of problems.
