# Reasoning: Mathematical Suppression of Unique Signals in Trifuse

## 1. Problem Identification
The Trifuse framework claims to "preserve strong and discriminative responses unique to individual modalities" via its **Single-Peak (SP)** fusion component. However, a mathematical audit of Equation 11 reveals a **Redundancy Paradox**: the mechanism actually penalizes unique peaks that lack support from other modalities, while only amplifying consensus peaks.

## 2. Mathematical Proof of Suppression
Equation 11 defines the confidence $\text{conf}_{s,j}$ of modality $s$ at position $j$ as:
$$ \text{conf}_{s,j} = \sigma \left( \alpha \cdot \frac{\sum_{s' \neq s} a_j^{s'}}{a_j^s + \varepsilon} - \beta \right) $$
where $\alpha=10, \beta=2$.

### Case A: The "Unique Peak" (Goal of SP)
Suppose modality $s$ (e.g., OCR) correctly identifies a small button with high confidence $a_j^s = 1.0$, while the other two modalities (Attention and Caption) missed it ($a_j^{s'} \approx 0$).
- Ratio $\approx 0 / 1 = 0$
- $\text{conf}_{s,j} = \sigma(10 \cdot 0 - 2) = \sigma(-2) \approx 0.12$
- Weight $W_{s,j} = 1 + 0.5(2 \cdot 0.12 - 1) = 1 - 0.38 = 0.62$
**Result:** The unique correct signal is **suppressed by ~38%**.

### Case B: The "Consensus Peak"
Suppose all three modalities agree ($a_j^s = 1.0$ for all).
- Ratio $= 2 / 1 = 2$
- $\text{conf}_{s,j} = \sigma(10 \cdot 2 - 2) = \sigma(18) \approx 1.0$
- Weight $W_{s,j} = 1 + 0.5(2 \cdot 1 - 1) = 1.5$
**Result:** The consensus signal is **amplified by 50%**.

## 3. Contradiction with Claims
The paper's prose (L955-L956) states that SP "emphasizes reliable peaks while softly suppressing isolated noisy responses." However, the framework has no mechanism to distinguish an "isolated noisy response" from an "isolated correct unique response." By design, it assumes agreement is the only marker of reliability, which makes the SP term redundant with the Consensus term (the element-wise product).

## 4. Target Absence Blindness
The localization logic (L506) selects the global maximum of the fused heatmap:
$$ \text{idx} = \text{argmax}(\mathbf{M}^{\text{final}}) $$
As acknowledged in L930-L931 and L1208, the model does not support "Refusal" (returning -1, -1). This forces the model to zoom in on noise or unrelated elements when the target is missing, a critical failure mode for autonomous agents.

## 5. Resolution
To resolve the Redundancy Paradox, the confidence weight should be decoupled from cross-modal agreement, for example by using the internal entropy or local peakiness of the individual modality's heatmap. To resolve Target Absence Blindness, a learned or heuristic threshold on the maximum response value is required to enable abstention.
