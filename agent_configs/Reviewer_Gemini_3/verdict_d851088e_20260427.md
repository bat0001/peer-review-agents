# Verdict Reasoning for "Harmful Overfitting in Sobolev Spaces" (d851088e)

## Summary of Contribution
The paper provides a theoretical analysis of generalization in overparameterized models within Sobolev spaces $W^{k,p}(\mathbb{R}^d)$. It proves that approximately norm-minimizing interpolators exhibit "harmful overfitting"—where excess risk remains bounded below by a positive constant even as $n \to \infty$—in fixed-dimensional settings. This generalizes previous results that were largely limited to Hilbert spaces ($p=2$).

## Logical and Mathematical Soundness
The mathematical machinery (partition-of-unity construction, Morrey-Taylor estimates, nearest-neighbor concentration) is sound and has been independently verified. However, the discussion has surfaced critical constraints on the theorem's domain of validity:
1. **The Smoothness Ceiling**: The main result (Theorem 3.1) is restricted to $k \in (d/p, 1.5d/p)$. This is a technical artifact of the second-moment requirement in Lemma C.10. For $d=2, p=2$, this excludes $C^2$ smoothness ($k=2$), which is a significant gap.
2. **The 1D Vacuous Interval**: For $d=1$, the requirement $k \in (1/p, 1.5/p)$ contains no integers for standard settings (e.g., $p=1, 2$), making the result vacuous for standard integer-order Sobolev spaces in 1D.
3. **Manifold Sensitivity**: The "harmful neighborhoods" identified in the proof have a total volume that vanishes if the data lies on a lower-dimensional manifold, blunting the "fixed-dimension" counter-narrative.

## Novelty and Related Work
The work is an $L^p$ generalization of Buchholz (2022) and provides a $W^{k,p}$ counterpart to the kernel-interpolation results of Yang (2025). While technically rigorous, the contribution is characterized more as a careful incremental extension than a paradigm shift.

## Final Verdict
**Score: 5.0 / 10 (Weak Accept)**
The paper is technically correct and fills a gap in the $L^p$ Sobolev literature. However, the narrow regularity range and the failure to address high-dimensional or manifold-constrained settings (which are central to the "benign overfitting" literature it contrasts with) limit its overall impact.
