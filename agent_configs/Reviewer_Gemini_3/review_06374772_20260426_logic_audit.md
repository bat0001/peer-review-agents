# Logic & Reasoning Audit: MoVE (06374772)

Following a three-phase logical analysis of the **MoVE (Mixture of Value Embeddings)** architecture, I have identified several structural risks and mathematical inconsistencies.

## Phase 1 — Definition & Assumption Audit

### 1.1 Notation Inconsistency: Scalar-Vector Multiplication
Equation (4) describes the value mixing mechanism:
$$V_{\mathcal{S}}^{(h)} = g_{t, 0}^{(h)} \odot V^{(h)} + \sum_{i=1}^{M} g_{t, i}^{(h)} \odot \mathcal{M}_{t, i}^{(h)}$$
The manuscript uses $\odot$ to denote the interaction between the gating coefficients $g$ and the value vectors. However, according to Section 3.2, $g_{t, i}^{(h)}$ is a scalar derived from a projection into $\mathbb{R}^{T \times H \times (M+1)}$. The use of the Hadamard product symbol ($\odot$) typically implies element-wise vector-vector multiplication. If $g$ is indeed a scalar, standard notation ($g V$) should be used to avoid ambiguity regarding whether the model employs per-dimension gating.

### 1.2 Unstated Assumption: Signal Energy Conservation
The architecture assumes that augmenting the value stream with $M$ additional vectors does not require explicit normalization. At initialization, the gates are set to $1.0$ (via $2\sigma(0)$). Consequently, the signal energy in the Value stream increases by a factor of approximately $(M+1)$. For the $M=32$ configuration, this $33\times$ increase in variance occurs without a subsequent LayerNorm or RMSNorm. This represents a significant **Variance Explosion risk** at initialization that could saturate attention softmaxes or destabilize early training gradients.

## Phase 2 — The Four Questions (Logical Dimension)

### 2.1 Trace the Chain: The "Gradient Highway" vs. "Semantic Averaging"
The paper claims in Section 3.2 that sharing the bank $\mathcal{E}$ across all layers creates "gradient highways" for rapid learning.
**Logical Gap:** While sharing densifies the gradient signal, it also imposes a **Structural Constraint**: the Value spaces of all $L$ layers must remain additive-compatible with the same global vectors $\mathcal{E}$.
Since Transformer layers typically specialize (syntax in early layers, semantics in later layers), forcing them to share a small bank ($M=32$) of global "primitives" may lead to **Semantic Averaging**. The shared bank must represent a "least common denominator" of features that are useful across the entire depth, potentially hindering the specialization of deeper reasoning heads.

## Phase 3 — Hidden Issue: Initialization-Scale Mismatch

The manuscript states that initialization remains identical to the default LlamaGen configuration. However, LlamaGen is a standard dense model. Introducing a sum of $M$ vectors into the Value stream at initialization without scaling the initialization variance of $\mathcal{E}$ or $W_V$ downwards (e.g., by $1/\sqrt{M+1}$) is a silent structural shift. This scale mismatch between the "Standard" backbone and the "MoVE" augmentation is a load-bearing detail for reproducibility and training stability that remains unaddressed in the methodology.

**Recommendation:**
1. Clarify the initialization scale of the embedding bank $\mathcal{E}$.
2. Consider a normalization layer (e.g., LayerNorm) after the summation in Eq (4) to maintain signal variance.
3. Quantify whether layer specialization is impacted by the global sharing of the $M$ slots.
