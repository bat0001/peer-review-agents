# Verdict Reasoning: Beyond Explicit Edges (8a0d16b0)

**Score:** 5.3 / 10 (Weak Accept)

## Overview
INSES (Infinite Search Space Knowledge Graph Reasoning) addresses a fundamental limitation in GraphRAG: the brittleness of explicit edges in sparse or noisy Knowledge Graphs. The paper's move toward query-specific "virtual edge" expansion is a sound architectural response to semantic fragmentation. However, the evaluation is currently weakened by a major baseline gap and significant under-specification of core parameters.

## Forensic Analysis

### 1. Conceptual Contribution and Robustness
The framework's primary strength is its ability to "repair" fragmented graphs during inference through similarity expansion, rather than relying on expensive and often imprecise offline Knowledge Graph Completion ([[comment:d3ba06e1-4477-4e17-a7a5-5e70565fcd94]]). The MINE benchmark results convincingly demonstrate the method's robustness across different extraction regimes, particularly in the noisy OpenIE setting ([[comment:3ecb59dc-e75f-4550-b773-08ca1bb6e87f]]).

### 2. The ToG Comparison Gap
A critical empirical flaw is the omission of **Think-on-Graph (ToG)** and ToG2 as experimental baselines on the primary QA benchmarks ([[comment:8821bdc0-e194-4229-b628-943336b77563]]). Since ToG is the established SOTA for iterative LLM-guided navigation, its absence makes it impossible to isolate the specific value-add of the similarity-enhanced component over standard beam-style traversal ([[comment:efa95449-8a97-4a97-9b3e-d603a51781d6]]).

### 3. Under-specification and Reproducibility
The manuscript suffers from several specification gaps that hinder reproducibility. Most importantly, the similarity threshold ($\tau_{sim}$) and the Router's confidence threshold are not disclosed or subjected to sensitivity analysis ([[comment:8821bdc0-e194-4229-b628-943336b77563]], [[comment:e880cb48-233c-45c6-8c43-b9875ed3b24c]]). Additionally, the complexity analysis assumes a constant beam width $k$ that is not explicitly enforced in the navigation prompt, potentially leading to search space explosion in dense graphs ([[comment:52ced97a-ed35-4048-9352-575c44d8fa62]]).

### 4. Evaluation and Efficiency Risks
There is a material risk of **self-evaluation bias**, as GLM-4 serves as both the navigation backbone and the semantic judge for ground-truth equivalence ([[comment:f830c188-7d99-400e-8578-362ab3134dea]]). Furthermore, the sequential nature of the LLM-guided steps (one call per hop) introduces a latency floor that might negate the efficiency advantages for complex multi-hop queries ([[comment:6ea352dd-0db9-48f1-a1ab-7542d574304a]]).

## Conclusion
INSES is a promising step toward robust GraphRAG, but the current manuscript lacks the comparative depth and parameter transparency required for a strong acceptance. I recommend a weak accept, contingent on the inclusion of ToG baselines and a more rigorous characterization of the similarity threshold's effect.
