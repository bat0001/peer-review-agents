# Forensic Verdict: 7c38c3a4 (TAB-PO: Token-Level Preference Optimization)

**Score:** 4.5 (Weak Reject)

## 1. Rationale
TAB-PO addresses a critical failure mode in structured generation: the gradient dilution of semantic tokens (labels/spans) within shared structural scaffolding (JSON). The incorporation of expert-curated "hard negatives" and a token-adaptive barrier is a well-motivated systems response. However, the manuscript's empirical claims are forensically weakened by relaxed evaluation standards and reporting inconsistencies:

1. **Theoretical Margin Inflation:** The span evaluation metric (Appendix \ref{append:metric}) allows for "full containment," meaning a model that extracts an entire paragraph as a span is credited with a True Positive for a gold sub-span. This extraordinarily permissive logic eliminates the penalty for boundary noise, rendering the claimed improvements in "grounding precision" unverifiable.
2. **High-Capacity Trade-offs:** Diagnostic analysis of Table 2 reveals a Span F1 regression on the largest model (Llama-3.3-70B). This suggests that the token-weighted preference signal, while improving categorical labels, may be trading off character-exact grounding precision in high-capacity regimes—a dynamic the authors do not sufficiently characterize.
3. **Inconsistent Result Reporting:** The manuscript contains conflicting headline figures, reporting relative improvements of ~4%, ~4.8%, and ~6.9% across different sections (Abstract, Intro, Contributions). This suggests a lack of systematic cross-verification during final manuscript preparation.
4. **Baseline & Scope Gaps:** The evaluation ignores established token-level DPO neighbors (TDPO, TI-DPO) and is restricted to a single clinical dataset (PV-Miner), leaving the generalizability of the method to other structured tasks (function calling, NER) unproven.

## 3. Recommended Resolution
A revision must:
1. Report Exact Match (EM) Span F1 scores to validate true grounding precision.
2. Resolve the numeric inconsistencies in headline reporting.
3. Include a comparison with at least one token-importance DPO baseline (e.g., TI-DPO).
4. Demonstrate generalizability on a second structured generation task (e.g., Berkeley Function Calling Leaderboard).
