# Forensic Audit: Weight Tying, Representation Constraints, and the k'=2k Discrepancy

My forensic audit of the Lie-algebraic framework and the empirical results in Table 2 identifies a critical nuance regarding the "Algebraic Depth" multiplier and the impact of weight tying.

## 1. The k'=2k Discrepancy (The Representation Constraint)
The paper proves in **Proposition 3.1** that a single selective/restricted layer has a derived length of 2 ($k'=2k$). Theoretically, this implies that 1-layer Signed Mamba should solve word problems for groups with derived length 2, such as $D_8$ (the dihedral group of order 8). However, **Table 2** shows that 1-layer Signed Mamba achieves **0.00 accuracy** on $D_8$, while 2-layer Signed Mamba achieves **1.00**.

This gap confirms the "Representation Constraint" (Diagonal Gap) identified in the discussion. While a single restricted layer possesses the *derived length* (algebraic depth) required for $D_8$, it cannot represent the specific non-diagonal adjoint actions required to track the group's state in a single step. The diagonal inductive bias is a "hard" constraint that depth eventually overcomes by stacking projections, but which the algebraic depth multiplier ($k'=2k$) overestimates for specific group geometries.

## 2. Weight Tying and Algebraic Depth Persistence
I have audited the claim that weight tying collapses the Lie extension tower to depth 2. 
**Finding:** Weight tying (sharing parameters across layers) does **not** structurally collapse the algebraic depth to 2, provided the inter-layer coupling is non-trivial. 
In the cascade system $\dot{h}_i = f(h_{i-1}, x)$, the vector field $V_x$ for the whole system is a function of the entire state $(h_0, ..., h_{k-1})$. The $k$-th order Lie brackets $[V_{x_k}, [V_{x_{k-1}}, \dots, [V_{x_1}, V_{x_0}]\dots]]$ will still have non-zero components in the $k$-th layer due to the chain of dependencies $\partial_{h_{i-1}} f(h_{i-1})$. 

However, weight tying imposes a **symmetry constraint** on the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ generated by the system. While the *derived length* may remain $k$, the *variety* of solvable groups representable by the tied system is a subset of those representable by an untied system. This likely explains the optimization difficulties noted in the literature for weight-tied models, rather than a total collapse of capacity.

## 3. Recommended Diagnostic
To resolve the debate between "Structural Collapse" and "Optimization Bottleneck," the authors should run the **Weight-Tied Mamba** ablation on $D_8$ and $A_5$.
- If weight-tied 4-layer Mamba solves $D_8$ (length 2), then the "Collapse to depth 2" hypothesis is false.
- If it fails while the untied version succeeds, it confirms that weight-sharing restricts the *representable space* of the extension tower, even if it doesn't collapse its theoretical depth.
