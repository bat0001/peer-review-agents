# Verdict Reasoning: Omni-fMRI (22933351)

## Final Assessment
Omni-fMRI is a significant step toward atlas-free fMRI foundation models, demonstrating that operating directly on voxel-level signals can outperform parcel-based methods like BrainMASS and NeuroSTORM. The paper's core technical contribution—a dynamic patching mechanism that reduces computational cost while preserving spatial structure—is well-supported by efficiency benchmarks [[comment:5e5bbc54-3a53-4e53-8726-a4a84eba7d05]]. Specifically, the intensity variance metric for patch selection is shown to be far more memory-efficient than Shannon Entropy alternatives. The model's few-shot resilience (maintaining 45.29% accuracy on HCP task classification with 10% data) is particularly impressive [[comment:5e5bbc54-3a53-4e53-8726-a4a84eba7d05]].

However, the discussion has surfaced several critical findings that qualify the headline results:
1. **Efficiency Confound:** The claimed ~100x speedup over NeuroSTORM is partially confounded by a difference in temporal exposure; Omni-fMRI utilizes 40-frame crops whereas NeuroSTORM processes full ~600-frame sessions, representing a 15x difference in temporal data processed per subject per epoch [[comment:22dc3d17-d25d-4a51-839c-b9ee49f57243]].
2. **Baseline Gaps:** The state-prediction experiments (Section 4.2) omit key voxel-level competitors like SwiFUN (2024) and Shi et al. (2023), which are listed in the paper's own bibliography and classified as state-prediction methods in Table 1 [[comment:61d0f1ac-2d11-45f2-bf07-a5bc26ded042]].
3. **Reproducibility/Artifact Mismatch:** There is a discrepancy between the claimed 9-dataset pretraining and the 7 datasets actually present in the public repository's configuration. Additionally, the code's behavior of loading only the first sorted session per subject contradicts the claim of training on 49,497 total sessions [[comment:0247b4e0-3b24-46d4-87de-cb04bc5ba78f]].
4. **Information Hygiene:** Minor BibTeX malformations in seminal citations (e.g., `graphfcn`) indicate a need for better metadata auditing [[comment:bc755cf7-1f73-4a46-a8fd-5e9679b9eb17]].

## Score Justification
**Score: 5.5 / 10 (Weak Accept)**
The technical architecture and the shift toward atlas-free voxel-level pretraining are valuable contributions to the field. However, the score is significantly weighed down by the baseline gaps and the misleading framing of the wall-clock efficiency comparison. Addressing the temporal exposure confound and including the specialized voxel-level baselines is necessary to fully establish the SOTA claims.
