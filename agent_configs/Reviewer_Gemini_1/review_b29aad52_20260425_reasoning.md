## Phase 1 — Foundation Audit

### 1.1 Novelty Verification
RetroReasoner proposes a dual-stage SFT+RL pipeline for retrosynthesis. The use of "SyntheticRetro" rationales generated by a larger model (distillation of reasoning) is a notable application of chain-of-thought to the chemical domain.

### 1.2 Reproducibility Note
The reliance on a proprietary model (GPT-oss-20B) for generating the training rationales creates a significant barrier to independent reproduction of the training set.

## Phase 2 — The Four Questions

### 2.1 Problem Identification
Standard retrosynthesis models predict reactants as a single-step translation, bypassing the intermediate "strategic" decisions (e.g., bond disconnection) that chemists use.

### 2.2 Relevance
High. Efficient retrosynthesis planning is a foundational bottleneck in drug discovery and chemical manufacturing.

### 2.3 Claim vs. Reality
- **Claim:** "Strategic reasoning" improves performance on challenging instances.
- **Reality:** If the "reasoning" chains are not verified against chemical first principles, the model may be learning a **template-retrieval heuristic** dressed in reasoning vocabulary.

## Phase 3 — Hidden-Issue Checks

### 3.1 Reward Circularity and "Model Hacking"
**Finding:** The RL stage uses a forward synthesis model ($f_\phi$) to provide the "round-trip" reward.
**Forensic Analysis:** If $f_\phi$ is trained on the same reaction databases (ORDerly/USPTO) as the generator, it likely shares the same systematic biases. The RL process optimizes for the **generator-verifier joint distribution** rather than chemical truth. The model can achieve high "round-trip accuracy" by proposing reactants that the forward model *incorrectly* believes lead to the product. Without an independent, rule-based (e.g., RDKit-based) or physics-based (e.g., DFT) validator, the RL loop is a self-reinforcing echo chamber of the underlying dataset's noise.

### 3.2 The Stereochemistry Gap
**Finding:** The abstract and methodology do not explicitly mention the handling of stereoisomers or atom-mapping consistency.
**Forensic Analysis:** Organic synthesis is fundamentally three-dimensional. A bond-disconnection "strategy" that ignores chiral centers and stereochemical inversions is chemically incomplete. If the "rationales" operate only on 1D SMILES/SELFIES without tracking stereochemical trajectories, the "reasoning" is a simplified abstraction that will fail in high-complexity drug discovery where enantiomeric purity is mandatory.

### 3.3 Fragment Coherence in "Synthon Mapping"
**Finding:** The "SyntheticRetro" framework maps disconnections to synthons and then to reagents.
**Forensic Analysis:** The mapping from a synthon (an idealized fragment) to a reagent (a commercially available molecule) is a non-trivial jump. I flag this step as a potential source of **hallucinated chemistry**. I will audit whether the framework ensures that predicted reagents are actually compatible with the proposed bond disconnection, or if it merely predicts "plausible-looking" molecules that the forward model happens to accept.
