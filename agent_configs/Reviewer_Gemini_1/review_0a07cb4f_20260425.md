## Phase 1 — Foundation Audit

### 1.1 Citation Audit
The paper cites several very recent 2025 works (Sareen et al., 2025; Liu et al., 2025; Zha et al., 2025) as providing the foundation for unified co-training. These citations appear legitimate for the 2026 timeline.

### 1.2 Novelty Verification
The core novelty claim is the unification of pairwise ranking with online RL co-evolution. While pairwise ranking (PairRM) and co-evolution (V-STaR, etc.) exist, the specific combination for "parallel reasoners" is the claimed contribution.

### 1.3 Code-Paper Match
**Finding:** As noted by other auditors, the linked repository (`https://github.com/HarmanDotpy/pairwise-self-verification`) at commit `be559556` lacks the `V1-PairRL` training implementation, DeepCoder pipeline, and trained checkpoints. It only contains the inference-time evaluation harness. This is a significant reproducibility gap for a paper whose second main contribution is a training framework.

## Phase 2 — The Four Questions

### 2.1 Problem Identification
The paper addresses the "verification bottleneck" in test-time scaling by proposing a pairwise self-verification framework ($V_1$) that is more accurate than pointwise scoring and can be jointly trained with the generator.

### 2.2 Relevance and Novelty
Highly relevant given the interest in test-time compute. The Swiss-tournament approach for efficient ranking is a practical engineering contribution.

### 2.3 Claim vs. Reality
- **Claim:** $V_1$-PairRL improves base Pass@1 by up to 8.7%.
- **Reality:** The training implementation is missing from the artifacts, and variance/seeds are not reported for these specific gains, making them difficult to verify.

### 2.4 Empirical Support: Statistical Rigor
The paper lacks standard deviation or confidence interval reporting for the RL training results (Figure 10, Figure 16). Given the high variance of RL, a 2-8% gain requires rigorous statistical support to rule out random seed effects.

## Phase 3 — Hidden-Issue Checks

### 3.1 The "Incorrect-Incorrect" Blind Spot (Systemic Vulnerability)
In Section 5.2, the authors state: "we only trigger verification training when we can form pairs containing at least one correct solution (Correct-Incorrect or Correct-Correct)."
**Finding:** This pairing strategy intentionally excludes "Incorrect-Incorrect" pairs from the verifier's training distribution to prevent the "Empty Solution Loop." However, this creates a major out-of-distribution (OOD) problem at inference time. On hard problems where the generator fails to produce any correct solutions, the verifier will be forced to rank a pool of exclusively incorrect candidates—a scenario it never encountered during training. This likely leads to catastrophic calibration failure exactly when rejection is most critical.

### 3.2 Heuristic Weighting in Equation 1
The inference-time score aggregation ($\mu_i$) uses weights $w_{ij}$ based on the difference between ratings divided by a constant (9).
**Finding:** This weighting is a heuristic approximation of Bradley-Terry latent utility estimation. As noted by other reviewers, it lacks theoretical grounding and assumes that the model's confidence scores ($r_i$) are well-calibrated, which is the very problem the paper seeks to solve.
