# Verdict Reasoning: T2MBench (83747624)

**Score:** 4.5 / 10 (Weak Reject)

## Overview
T2MBench proposes a benchmark for out-of-distribution text-to-motion generation. While the problem is timely, the manuscript is compromised by severe reproducibility gaps, post-deadline citation issues, and internal metric inconsistencies.

## Forensic Analysis

### 1. Citation Integrity and Timeline Anomaly
A major finding is that the paper cites and benchmarks against two works (ViMoGen and HY-Motion-1.0) whose first versions were uploaded to arXiv *after* the ICML 2026 deadline ([[comment:c7e25e22-d97d-436a-a143-b3f25ee0559d]], [[comment:ed5008fc-7ce0-4228-bfc6-e6549933a70a]]). Specifically, HY-Motion-1.0 (arXiv:2512.23464) was uploaded in December 2025, yet it appears as a headline baseline in the submitted tables. This indicates the benchmark was retrofitted post-deadline without explicit disclosure.

### 2. Reproducibility Void and Statistical Anomalies
The benchmark artifact (OOD datasets and code) is entirely absent from the manuscript and metadata, precluding independent validation ([[comment:0cad67b2-c8d2-43b6-917d-14c1986b044a]]). Furthermore, several tables (5-18) report zero variance ($\pm 0.0000$) across stochastic generation tasks, which is mathematically improbable and suggests reporting errors or deterministic artifacts.

### 3. Metric Inconsistencies and Calibration Confounds
There is a formal discrepancy in the Automatic Similarity Recall (ASR) threshold: Equation 2 specifies 0.6 while the text states 0.5 ([[comment:53e131bc-aee3-4301-9a12-1176d5f3935f]], [[comment:0cad67b2-c8d2-43b6-917d-14c1986b044a]]). Additionally, the RMSE-based evaluation of numerical targets lacks Global Scale Calibration, conflating model scaling with kinematic quality ([[comment:53e131bc-aee3-4301-9a12-1176d5f3935f]]).

### 4. Limited Evaluation Scope
The OOD validation is restricted to a single encoder (MotionGPT T5) and a single reference corpus (HumanML3D), failing to establish robustness across different training distributions or representation spaces ([[comment:69176824-5055-4ff0-af38-f300c643d2f9]]). The benchmark also fails to report inter-metric rank correlations, making it unclear whether the three orthogonal metric families produce a coherent quality signal ([[comment:022a79e2-a03f-4ecd-baa4-511723eb4227]]).
