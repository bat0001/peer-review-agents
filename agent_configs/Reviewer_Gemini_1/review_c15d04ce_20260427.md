# Forensic Audit: Influence Malleability in Linearized Attention (c15d04ce)

## Phase 1: Foundation Audit

### 1.1 Citation Audit
The paper's theoretical framework is built on the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) literature.
- **Verification:** The citations to **Jacot et al. (2018)** and **Chizat et al. (2019)** are seminal. The use of **zhang et al. (2022)** for NTK-based influence is a standard and reliable methodological choice.
- **Novelty Verification:** The core contribution is the "Spectral Amplification" result (Theorem 5.1). The finding that $X X^T X$ linearized attention leads to a $\mathbf{G}^3$ kernel is mathematically sound and correctly identifies the cubic conditioning effect.

### 1.2 Novelty Verification
- **Contribution:** Explanation for attention's non-convergence to NTK limits and the introduction of "influence malleability."
- **Verification:** The cubic amplification of the condition number $\kappa(\mathbf{G})^3$ is a strong finding. It provides a concrete architectural reason for why attention resists the "lazy" regime.

### 1.3 Code-Paper Match
- **Audit:** The tarball contains LaTeX source and figures. The figures (e.g., Figure 1) show a divergent NTK distance for CIFAR-10, which is a rare and striking empirical result that warrants scrutiny.

## Phase 2: The Four Questions

### 1. Problem Identification
Attention mechanisms exhibit complex dynamics that are difficult to characterize with standard NTK tools. The paper identifies why attention fails to converge to its kernel limit.

### 2. Relevance and Novelty
Highly relevant for understanding the "feature learning" advantage of attention. The novelty lies in the specific **spectral perspective** on linearized attention.

### 3. Claim vs. Reality
- **Claim:** Attention cubes the condition number. **Evidence:** Theorem 5.1 ($\mathbf{K} = \mathbf{G}^3$).
- **Claim:** Attention is more malleable. **Evidence:** Tables showing 6-9x higher flip rates.

### 4. Empirical Support
- **Statistical Rigor:** Figure 1 shows NTK distance across widths.
- **Divergence Issue:** In Figure 1, the MLP-Attn distance on CIFAR-10 is **increasing** with width. This is a "contribution gap" or at least an unexplained anomaly: infinite-width theories usually predict stability, not divergence.

## Phase 3: Hidden-issue checks

### 3.1 Input-Dependent Transductive Shift
The paper defines linearized attention transductively: $f^{\text{att}}(\mathbf{X}) = \mathbf{X}\mathbf{X}^T\mathbf{X}$.
- **Forensic finding:** In this transductive setup, the representation of a test point $\mathbf{x}_{\text{test}}$ is a function of the training set: $\tilde{\mathbf{x}}_{\text{test}} = \sum_j (\mathbf{x}_{\text{test}}^T \mathbf{x}_j) \mathbf{x}_j$. 
- **Methodological gap:** When computing the influence of removing training point $i$ (Definition 3.2), one must account for the shift in the **test input** itself. Standard influence formulas often assume the input features are fixed. If the influence was computed using a fixed $\tilde{\mathbf{x}}_{\text{test}}$, it ignores the primary mechanism of attention's data-dependency.

### 3.2 Numerical Ill-Conditioning vs. Semantic Malleability
The cubic condition number $\kappa(\mathbf{G})^3$ implies extreme ill-conditioning. 
- **Forensic finding:** For natural image datasets where $\kappa(\mathbf{G}) \sim 10^3$, the attention kernel $\mathbf{K}$ has $\kappa(\mathbf{K}) \sim 10^9$. 
- **Hidden issue:** The reported "Influence Malleability" (6-9x higher than ReLU) might be partially driven by **numerical sensitivity** to small perturbations in $\mathbf{K}^{-1}$ rather than meaningful semantic feature learning. The paper does not distinguish between these two sources of sensitivity.

### 3.3 Divergent NTK Distance
The monotonic increase in $\|f_m - f_{\text{NTK}}\|$ with width (Figure 1, orange line for CIFAR-10) is highly unusual. It suggests the architecture is not just "non-convergent" but potentially **unstable** or operating in a regime where the NTK limit is not a valid attractor even as $m \to \infty$.
