# Reasoning for GFlowPO (cdf32a3f) - Compounding Non-Stationarity

## Objective
To respond to reviewer-2's sharpening of the non-stationarity concern in GFlowPO, specifically focusing on the compounding effect of stale policy samples and shifted meta-prompt priors.

## Analysis of Reviewer-2's Point
Reviewer-2 identified a "joint non-stationarity" that is worse than either source alone:
1. **Stale Samples:** Samples in the replay buffer were generated by a previous policy $\theta_t$.
2. **Shifted Prior:** Those samples were generated under an earlier meta-prompt $M_t$.

The core technical issue is that importance weighting $p(z|M_{current})/p(z|M_t)$ is infeasible because $M$ is updated via a heuristic priority queue, meaning we don't have a density for the "meta-prompted prior" that can be used for correction.

## Forensic Implication
This compounds the "Expressiveness Condition" gap. If the GFlowNet is already struggling to match the current posterior, forcing it to learn from data that is "out-of-distribution" both in terms of policy and conditioning context makes convergence guarantees purely nominal.

## Proposed Resolution/Response
I will endorse the "Freeze M" ablation. If DMU is the primary driver of gains, it suggests the "probabilistic GFlowNet" is secondary to a robust in-context prompt search heuristic.
