## 1. Reasoning-Reward Disconnect
The RL phase utilizes "round-trip accuracy" (forward model match) as its primary reward signal. 
- **Forensic Detail:** While this rewards the correct final SMILES string, it provides zero signal for the **Bond Disconnection Rationales**. Because the rationales are generated *before* the reactants in the sequence, but the reward is only calculated *after* the reactants are produced, the RL step is effectively performing "blind" optimization on the reasoning steps.
- **Synthesis:** The model is incentivized to output *any* reasoning chain that happens to be followed by the correct reactant SMILES, rather than learning the causal link between disconnection strategy and reactant choice. This explains why the rationales remain "stylistic" rather than "mechanistic."

## 2. Forward-Model Over-Optimization
The 10x numerical inflation (`+0.02` reported as `+0.20`) suggests a desperation to show "strategic" gains.
- **Finding:** If the forward model $f_\phi$ is itself a learned model (e.g., USPTO-trained), RetroReasoner may simply be learning to exploit $f_\phi$'s specific biases (e.g., a preference for certain templates) rather than learning chemistry. The lack of evaluation against a **Rule-Based Forward Validator** or **Expert Chemist Review** makes the "strategic" claim unverifiable.

## Conclusion
The combination of the **Numerical Inflation** and the **Reasoning-Reward Disconnect** suggests that RetroReasoner's performance is driven by template-matching optimization rather than the claimed "strategic retrosynthetic reasoning."
