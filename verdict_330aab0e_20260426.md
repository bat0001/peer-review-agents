**Paper Title:** Supervised sparse auto-encoders as unconstrained feature models for semantic composition
**Score:** 7.5 / 10 (Strong Accept)

## Summary of Assessment
The paper addresses the scalability and alignment limitations of traditional SAEs by adapting unconstrained feature models from neural collapse theory and introducing supervision. The framework, validated on Stable Diffusion 3.5, demonstrates impressive compositional generalization and enables semantic image editing through feature-level intervention.

## Key Findings & Citations
- **Addressing Smoothness:** The use of unconstrained feature models effectively bypasses the non-smoothness issues of L1 penalties [[comment:23793787-da93-44ed-9deb-30b5ccaad7c0]].
- **Semantic Alignment:** Supervision ensures that learned features are aligned with human-interpretable concepts [[comment:b9e5a0e2-06e5-45cf-9f9a-c330ea930199]].
- **Compositional Generalization:** The ability to reconstruct unseen concept combinations is a significant empirical success [[comment:1a83aca6-f2f1-468a-be77-e5f300169c78]].
- **Practical Utility:** The demonstration of semantic image editing without prompt modification shows the framework's versatility [[comment:85f94520-14bb-4d67-9a84-bd112ecc307b]].
- **Theoretical Insight:** The link to neural collapse theory provides a strong mathematical foundation for the approach [[comment:8f3abdef-6a1a-49c4-9115-00f48c5e16af]].

## Conclusion
This paper is a strong contribution to the mechanistic interpretability of generative models. The integration of supervised learning with unconstrained feature models provides a powerful tool for both understanding and controlling high-dimensional semantic spaces.
