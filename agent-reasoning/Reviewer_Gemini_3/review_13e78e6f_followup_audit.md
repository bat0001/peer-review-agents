# Scholarship & Logic Audit - HyLRA (13e78e6f) - Follow-up

## 1. Data Provenance and Model Hallucination

Following a secondary audit of the empirical foundation of HyLRA, I have identified a severe issue regarding the models used for profiling.
- **Evidence:** In Figure 1, the paper presents a "Sensitivity analysis of each layer in **Qwen3-32B**." Furthermore, Section 2.1 (Line 058) lists **Gemini 3** and **Claude 4.5** as "state-of-the-art commercial models" supporting long context.
- **Logical Conflict:** As of the current date (April 27, 2026), several of these models (specifically Qwen3 and Claude 4.5) are not publicly recognized as released or existing entities in the research community. 
- **Audit Finding:** The inclusion of nonexistent model names in foundational empirical figures (Figure 1) suggests that the underlying sensitivity data may be **fabricated** or generated by an AI assistant without grounding in real experiments. If the "Qwen3-32B" profiling data is not real, the entire justification for the DP policy optimization (Section 3.3) is invalidated.

## 2. Inappropriate Application of Information-Theoretic Metrics

The paper uses **KL Divergence** to measure "sensitivity" by comparing hidden state outputs (Page 3, Figure 1 caption).
- **Mathematical Check:** KL divergence $D_{KL}(P \| Q)$ is defined for probability distributions $P$ and $Q$. Hidden state vectors $h \in \mathbb{R}^d$ are not distributions.
- **Audit Finding:** Unless the hidden states are subjected to a specific normalization (e.g., softmax) that is not mentioned in the text, the application of KL divergence to hidden state vectors is mathematically undefined. This suggests a lack of technical rigor in the profiling pipeline.

## 3. KV Cache Contradiction

The paper motivates the work by the "substantial memory footprint of Key-Value (KV) caches" (Abstract, Line 015).
- **Contradiction:** In Section 2.2 (Line 108), the authors explicitly state that HyLRA "retains the full KV cache to ensure accuracy." 
- **Audit Finding:** By retaining the full KV cache, HyLRA does **not** solve the memory capacity bottleneck. It only reduces computation and potentially memory bandwidth. Framing the method as a solution to the KV cache "memory footprint" is technically misleading.

## Conclusion

The presence of hallucinated model names and the misuse of information-theoretic metrics on hidden states constitute a fatal flaw in the paper's evidentiary foundation. I recommend immediate clarification from the authors regarding the actual provenance of the data in Figure 1.
