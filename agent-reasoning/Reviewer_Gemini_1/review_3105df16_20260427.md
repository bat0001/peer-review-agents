### 1.2 Novelty Verification
- The paper correctly identifies the state-of-the-art in inference-time alignment (DeAL, RBoN, Caution, HedgeTune).
- It distinguishes itself from `Caution` by focusing on **disagreement-grounded risk** rather than distributional uncertainty of a single reward model. This is a valid technical distinction.

### 1.3 Code-Paper Match
- **Finding:** No public repository is provided. This is a reproducibility concern for a method that claims to be "retraining-free" and easy to deploy.

## Phase 2: The Four Questions

1. **Problem identification:** Alignment methods typically optimize for mean preference, ignoring heterogeneity across users, which leads to brittle policies and over-optimization.
2. **Relevance and novelty:** Extremely relevant given the known limitations of RLHF/DPO. The entropic decoding approach is technically sound and theoretically grounded in KL-DRO.
3. **Claim vs. reality:**
    - **Finding:** There is a significant inconsistency in the definition of the primary `Tradeoff` metric. 
    - Section 5.1 (L842) defines $\mathrm{Tradeoff}_{\text{eval}}(s,y) := \hat\mu_{\text{eval}}(s,y) - \lambda \hat\sigma_{\text{sel}}(s,y)$, where $\hat\sigma_{\text{sel}}$ is the **perturbation-sensitivity proxy**.
    - However, Table 1/2 and Appendix H.8 (L2704) use $\hat\sigma_{\text{eval}}$, the **standard deviation across human judge ratings**.
    - Numerical verification of Table 2 shows that the `Tradeoff` values (e.g., 6.22 for Base) are indeed derived from the reported `Risk` (0.67) and `Human Score` (7.56) via $\mu - \lambda\sigma$. Given that the Base risk matches the human disagreement reported in Table 7 (0.65), it is highly likely that Table 2 reports human risk, making the definition in Section 5.1 a misleading typo.
4. **Empirical support:** The improvements of DARC-$\epsilon$ over baselines (including `Caution` and `DeAL`) are consistent across MT-Bench and AlpacaEval. The gains on the high-disagreement subset (Table 2) are particularly strong, showing a reduction in human disagreement from 1.00 to 0.65.

## Phase 3: Hidden-issue checks

- **Logical consistency:** The derivation of the entropic objective is standard, but the "calibration" between LCB and $\chi^2$-DRO in Appendix C is somewhat hand-wavy regarding the interpretation of $n$ (sample size) when the "samples" are perturbations rather than independent raters.
- **Proxy Validity:** The paper acknowledges that the proxy has False Positives (Appendix app:proxy_mismatch, e.g., the "Race riddle" case where RM sensitivity is high but humans are unanimous). This reinforces the need for clarity on which $\sigma$ is used in the evaluation metrics.

## Conclusion

The paper presents a strong, Retraining-free method for risk-aware alignment. However, the inconsistency in the metric definition is a major clarity issue. If the authors are evaluating a proxy-based selection using the proxy itself in the metric, the result is biased. If they are using human ground truth (as the numbers suggest), the text needs to be corrected to reflect this.
