# Forensic Review: Discriminator-Driven Diffusion (a00312ed)

## Phase 1: Foundation Audit

### 1.1 Citation Audit
- **Findings:** Strong grounding in compositional diffusion (Decomp Diffusion, COMET). Sampled citations (DDPM, Decomp Diffusion) are correct.

### 1.2 Novelty Verification
- The addition of a discriminator to ensure realistic latent recombinations is a logical extension of the Decomp Diffusion framework. While somewhat incremental (mapping the GAN paradigm onto compositional diffusion), the specific formulation and the theoretical link to Cartesian-product support (Appendix H.2) add value.

### 1.3 Code-Paper Match
- Footnote 1 provides two anonymous science code repositories. These are common in blind reviews, though they were not accessible during the audit.

## Phase 2: The Four Questions

1. **Problem identification:** Unsupervised compositional models often produce artifacts when latent factors are recombined because the training objective (reconstruction) does not explicitly regularize the manifold of unseen factor combinations.
2. **Relevance and novelty:** Highly relevant for scalable and realistic compositional generation in vision and robotics.
3. **Claim vs. reality:** Claims improved FID across 4 datasets, improved disentanglement (MIG/MCC), and increased robotic state-space coverage. These claims are backed by Tables 1, 2, and 3.
4. **Empirical support:** Quantitative gains are substantial (e.g., 50% FID reduction on CelebA-HQ). The "inverted U-shaped" ablation on $\lambda$ (Figure 5) provides a realistic view of the method's sensitivity.

## Phase 3: Hidden-issue checks

- **Noise Conditioning Mismatch:** Algorithm 2 (Videos, Step 9-10) uses a shared noise input $x_t^A$ for both single-source and recombined predictions. As the authors correctly acknowledge in Appendix G, this means recombined predictions are conditioned on a noise realization tied to only one source image, which is not fully self-consistent with the generative process. 
- **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Table 3 shows that while $\lambda=0.001$ improves coverage, $\lambda=0.01$ actually causes it to drop below the baseline (9440 -> 7939 for Scene5). This indicates the method is highly sensitive to the discriminator weight and can easily over-regularize, collapsing factor semantics.
- **Algorithm Notation:** There is a minor notation mismatch between $\hat{x}_{\theta}$ (Eq. 6) and $\bar{G}_{\theta}$ (L524/Algorithm 2).

## Conclusion

The paper presents a well-motivated and empirically strong extension to unsupervised compositional diffusion. The theoretical grounding is elegant, and the acknowledgment of the noise conditioning limitation is commendable. However, the high sensitivity to the discriminator weight $\lambda$ is a practical concern for deployment.
