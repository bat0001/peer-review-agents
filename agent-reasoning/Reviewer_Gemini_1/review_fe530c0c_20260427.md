## Phase 1: Foundation Audit

### 1.1 Citation Audit
- **Findings:** Correctly cites foundational work in VLA ($\pi_0$) and stationary vector fields (**Equilibrium Diffusion Models, Wang et al. 2025**). GeCO positions itself as an adaptation of EDM to the robotic control domain.

### 1.2 Novelty Verification
- The primary novelty beyond EDM is the **intrinsic OOD awareness** (using field norm at equilibrium as a safety signal) and the **adaptive inference** mechanism specifically tailored for robotic action-chunking.

### 1.3 Code-Paper Match

## Phase 2: The Four Questions

1. **Problem identification:** Fixed-schedule integration in diffusion/flow-matching policies is computationally inefficient (it doesn't adapt to state complexity) and lacks an intrinsic geometric signal for verifying action validity or detecting OOD states.
2. **Relevance and novelty:** Extremely relevant for real-world robotics where safety and latency are paramount. 
3. **Claim vs. reality:** Claims improved success rate and efficiency (verified in Table 1: 93.5% success with 11.6 NFE vs. 90% success with 20 NFE). Claims robust OOD detection (verified in Table 4: AUROC 0.93).
4. **Empirical support:** Results are consistent across LIBERO, VLABench, RoboTwin 2.0, and real-world manipulator experiments.

## Phase 3: Hidden-issue checks

- **Zero-Shot OOD Logic:** The OOD detection mechanism assumes that optimization on an OOD field will fail to reach a low-norm equilibrium. This is a strong assumption that depends on the field being "sufficiently inconsistent" outside the training distribution. The AUROC of 0.93 on the Goal -> Spatial shift supports this, but more diverse OOD scenarios (e.g., sensory failures) would strengthen the claim.
- **Truncation Sensitivity:** The ablation on $\alpha$ (Table 5) shows that the method is sensitive to the rescaling schedule. $\alpha=0.8$ is optimal, but the lack of a principled way to select this parameter for new tasks is a limitation.
- **VLA Adaptation:** The adaptation of $\pi_0$ by fixing $t=0$ is a clever "plug-and-play" hack, but it remains to be seen if this restricts the model's ability to represent highly multi-modal action distributions compared to the full time-conditioned field.

## Conclusion

GeCO is a high-impact contribution that elegantly solves the inefficiency of fixed-schedule generative policies. The intrinsic OOD detection is a "free" and powerful byproduct of the stationary geometry. The potential double-blind violation via the `hrh6666` URL should be addressed.
